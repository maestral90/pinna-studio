Questions & Answers

Good to Know Before You Book

Is Charming Pinna Studio good for couples?

Yes. The studio sleeps two and can be set up as a double bed or as twin beds on request. Just mention your preference when booking. The central location is ideal for a romantic trip to Hvar: restaurants, the harbour, and the beach are all within a few minutes on foot, no car needed.

How close is it to the beach?

Franciscan Monastery Beach is about a 4 to 5 minute walk. Beach Bonj and access to the Pakleni Islands water taxi are under 1 km. Beach Krizna Luka is also reachable on foot. All beaches in Hvar Town are walkable from the studio.

How do I get from Split to the studio?

Take the catamaran from Split to Hvar Town (approximately 1 hour). TP Line runs more connections than Jadrolinija. On arrival, turn left at the port, walk straight, then left at the post office. The house is right next to the Mediterraneo restaurant. Airport transfer from Split Airport can also be arranged for an additional fee.

Is parking available?

There is no private parking at the property. Public parking is a 5-minute walk. Most guests arrive by catamaran from Split and don't need a car. Everything in Hvar old town is walkable.

Is the studio quiet at night?

The studio faces a small square with the Mediterraneo restaurant. Evenings in Hvar old town are lively, which is part of the charm of staying in the centre. The air conditioning means windows can stay closed on warm summer nights.

Is it a good base for day trips?

Yes. Water taxis to the Pakleni Islands leave nearby. Ferries to Brac and Korcula are accessible from the port. The bus station (5 min walk) connects to the rest of the island, including Stari Grad and Jelsa. The catamaran back to Split runs several times daily.

What languages does the host speak?

The host and concierge team speak English, Croatian, and German.

Where exactly is the studio located?

The address is Brace Bibic 7, 21450 Hvar. It is a private apartment in a building in Hvar old town, on a small square next to the Mediterraneo restaurant, less than one minute from the catamaran port and the main square.

Local Recommendations

A Day in Hvar, the Way We'd Spend It

Getting there: TP Line runs more connections to Hvar than Jadrolinija, with fast catamarans straight into Hvar town. Jadrolinija also runs catamarans, fewer of them, plus a car ferry to Stari Grad.

Morning

Start with good coffee

Begin the day with a proper coffee just up the street from the apartment. Kava37 does excellent specialty coffee, and Kantun next door is the spot for healthy snacks and fresh drinks. Both are a one-minute walk uphill.

Kava37 Kantun
Plan A Midday

Get out on the water

Head for the Pakleni Islands, the cluster of green islands in front of Hvar town. Jerolim is our pick. Palmizana is lush and laid-back, Carpe Diem Beach brings the day-club energy, and Mlini is quiet and beautiful. To explore the hidden coves, rent a boat with or without a skipper.

Jerolim Sego Rent Yachts
or
Plan B Midday

Or stay on Hvar

Not in the mood to island-hop? Spend the afternoon at Falko Beach Bar, tucked into the pines, where you can swim, lounge, and eat. As the light drops, head to Hula Hula for its sunset party: drinks, music, and a crowd facing west.

Falko Beach Bar
Afternoon

Something sweet

When a sweet craving hits between adventures, Nonica is the answer. Some of the best cakes and cookies on the island, perfect with the second coffee of the day.

Nonica
Evening

Dinner and after

Two good calls for dinner: Black Pepper, a reliable favourite, or the newly opened Bistro Yumo if you want something fresh. After dinner, head to Lola Bar for drinks and see where the evening goes.

Black Pepper Bistro Yumo
